Project Controls – Terms & Definitions

Fixed Fee (FF): A contract type where the service provider delivers the agreed scope of work for a fixed amount of compensation. The fee does not change based on actual costs incurred.

Transfer Pricing (TP): A pricing mechanism used for transactions between different legal entities within the same organization to allocate costs and revenues appropriately.

Borrowed and Lend (B&L): An internal cost recovery process where one cost center provides resources or services to another and transfers the associated costs.

Time and Materials (T&M): A contract arrangement where billing is based on actual time worked and materials consumed during project execution.

Direct Labour Multiplier (DLM): A metric used to calculate revenue derived from direct labour costs.

DLM = Net Revenue / Raw Labour

Days of Sales Outstanding (DSO): A financial metric that measures the average number of days required to collect payment after invoicing.

Inception to Date (ITD): Cumulative project data measured from the start of the project up to the current reporting period.

Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): A hierarchical breakdown of total project scope into manageable components used for planning, scheduling, and cost control.

Delegation of Authority (DOA): A framework defining approval authority levels and financial limits within the organization.

Raw Labour = Fringe + Overhead

Request for Qualification (RFQ): A document issued to evaluate vendor capability and pre‑qualify them before requesting detailed proposals.

Request for Proposal (RFP): A formal request issued to vendors asking for detailed technical and commercial proposals by a specified due date.

Best and Final Offer (BAFO): The final negotiated proposal submitted by vendors after evaluation and clarification rounds.

Resource Breakdown Structure (RBS): A classification of project resources such as labour, equipment, and materials, used for budgeting and forecasting.

Master Service Agreement (MSA): A governing contract that defines overarching legal and commercial terms for multiple projects or work orders.

Key Performance Indicator (KPI): A measurable value used to assess performance effectiveness against project or organizational objectives.

Key Risk Indicator (KRI): A metric used to identify and monitor risk trends based on leading or historical indicators.

Oracle Time and Labor (OTL): Oracle’s time recording system used for tracking employee work hours and feeding payroll, billing, and costing processes.

Estimate to Complete (ETC): The projected remaining cost required to complete all outstanding project work.

Estimate at Completion (EAC): The total forecasted cost of the project upon completion.

EAC = Actual Cost + Estimate to Complete

Margin Variance: The difference between actual margin and the originally planned margin.

Actual Net Revenue − (Actual Raw Labour × Original Budget Multiplier)

Raw Cost: The base cost calculated from effort data, derived using different logic depending on unit type such as hours, currency, each, or distance.

Raw Cost = SUMX(Effort Report logic based on unit type)
